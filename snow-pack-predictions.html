Neural Network Snow Pack Predictions

Using an LSTM to predict snow water equivalent (SWE) snow pack in the Wasatch mountains

  • back to main page

SWE is an important indicator of how much water the state of Utah will have access to throughout the spring, summer, and fall months.

In this notebook I import, clearn and format snowpack data collected throughout the state of Utah. I then train an LSTM model to use 30 days of past snow pack data to predict the expected snow pack 7 days into the future. The resulting model works well. Unsurprisingly it wold have predicted lower snow pack for the winter of 2022/23 than we actually received -- last winter was a banner year. The resulting root mean squared error (RMSE) is four inches, when the model makes predictions on data it has never seen during its training or validation cycles.
